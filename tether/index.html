Estimate Tether (USDT) carbon footprint on Ethereum platform

USDT is a stablecoin (stable-value cryptocurrency) that mirrors the price of the U.S. dollar, issued by a Hong Kong-based company Tether. The token’s peg to the USD is achieved via maintaining a sum of commercial paper, fiduciary deposits, cash, reserve repo notes, and treasury bills in reserves that is equal in USD value to the number of USDT in circulation.
Tether operates as a second-layer token on top of other cryptocurrencies’ blockchains: Bitcoin, Ethereum, EOS, Tron, Algorand, Bitcoin Cash and OMG, and is secured by their respective hashing algorithms.
At the moment, we only consider Tether’s carbon footprint on the Ethereum network. Emissions from other blockchains will be added later, after we have the emission estimate of the corresponding blockchain.

Methodology

Since Tether does not run its own chain and relies on the infrastructure of other chains, we allocate part of the total emissions of the underlying infrastructure blockchain to it and consider that as the emission it is responsible for.

Blockchain Metrics

We calculate circulating supply of USDT on Ethereum, transactions count and transaction fees involving USDT smart contract from Ethereum blockchain data in BigQuery public dataset

Circulating supply

We calculate USDT circulating supply by identifying USDT amounts associated with Issue, Redeem and DestroyedBlackFunds events of the smart contract, and aggregating the amounts to obtain daily supply.

Transaction count and transaction fees

There are two types of transactions interacting with Tether smart contract:
  1. The To field is the address of the smart contract address, e.g. https://etherscan.io/tx/0xa7c073e726ccdcffb906d9a3f6165a0ed994fcdd3d3156fb21e30978fc2947b0
  2. The smart contract address is not shown in the To field but it shows up in the log file. This happens when the interaction with Tether smart contract is part of a batch, e.g. a batch submitted via the Uniswap router.
