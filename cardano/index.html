Cardano Carbon Footprint Methodology

The emissions due to holding and transacting Cardano (ADA) are calculated using the methodology outlined here, with the following modifications, data sources, and assumptions.

Electricity Consumption

We calculate the total electricity consumption of the Cardano network by:
  • Estimating the average power consumption of a typical node
  • Taking the number of active node pools on each day
  • Multiplying by an estimate of 4 nodes per pool

Per-node electricity consumption

The Cardano documentation states the minimal hardware requirements are as follows:
Servers: 1 for block producer node, 1 for relay node
  • CPU: An Intel or AMD x86 processor with two or more cores, at 1.6GHz or faster (2GHz or faster for a stake pool or relay)
  • Memory: 16GB of RAM
  • Storage: 75GB of free storage (100GB recommended for future growth)
  • Operating system: 64-bit Linux (i.e. Ubuntu Server 20.04 LTS)

The ARM spec does not fulfill the recommended minimal hardware requirements and therefore we choose to exclude it from our analysis, and use the figure of 101.25W as the typical power consumption of a Cardano node.
As stated by Climate Neutral Cardano, a significant number of relays and nodes run on cloud servers and in data centers will operate at least 30% more energy-efficiently than standalone servers. We conservatively assume 25% of nodes are running in such a way, and consuming 30% less electricity.
This yields an average electricity consumption per node of 93.66W.

Nodes per pool

From Climate Neutral Cardano:
The operation of at least two independent (redundant) relays is recommended. In addition to publicly listed relays, which are officially registered, some pool operators have hidden (non-public) relays for security reasons. Their number is undetermined. In order to include them, a total number of 3 relays is assumed for each pool.
The total number of nodes (= block producers and relays) considered for the calculation is 4 for each pool (3 relays, 1 block producer).

Geographic distribution

According to Climate Neutral Cardano, most pools are located in Europe and the US.
This estimate was done in September 2021. Without further information, a reasonable assumption is that EU and US each take 50% of the pools.
cexplorer provides detailed statistics of all relays, including their IP addresses and corresponding geo location. As at 30 July 2022 this was their breakdown:
  • United States: 38.24%
  • Germany: 18.5%
  • Singapore: 8.77%
  • United Kingdom: 6.25%
  • Japan: 5.98%
  • Ireland: 4.61%
  • Finland: 3.8%
  • Canada: 2.54%
  • France: 2.43%
  • Netherlands: 2.1%
This shows a trend to shift to Asian countries such as Singapore and Japan. The shift of the computation power is gradual unless a certain country bans crypto completely or the energy price surges drastically in the short term.
High energy prices in the EU start to kick in since April 2022, however we do not hold any evidence relates this to the shift.
To be conservative, we assume the following computational power distribution starting from 1 July 2022.
  • Europe: 45%,
  • United States: 40%
  • Singapore: 9%
  • Japan: 6%
